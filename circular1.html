What is Circular Economy?

A circular economy is a systems-based approach to economic development that benefits businesses, society, and the environment. Unlike the traditional linear model of "take, make, dispose," the circular model is regenerative by design.

It aims to keep products, materials, and resources in use for as long as possible. This means designing out waste, reusing materials, and regenerating natural systems. It’s not just about recycling—it’s about rethinking the entire lifecycle of a product.

In a circular economy, products are designed to be durable, repairable, and upgradable. Businesses shift from selling products to offering services, and consumers become users who participate in sharing, leasing, and returning goods.

This model reduces pressure on the environment, improves resource efficiency, and opens up new economic opportunities. It’s a powerful strategy for tackling climate change, biodiversity loss, and pollution.

Governments, companies, and communities around the world are adopting circular principles—from zero-waste cities to circular fashion brands. It’s a global shift that requires innovation, collaboration, and systems thinking.

For youth, the circular economy is a chance to lead. Whether through school projects, startups, or community initiatives, young people can drive the transition to a regenerative future—one where nothing is wasted and everything has value.
